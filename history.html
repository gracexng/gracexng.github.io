HISTORY

The unique culinary art of yum cha emerged in China thousands of years ago with some sources saying it came about 2,500 years ago as evidenced in the poetry and music of that time. Originally it was an exclusive luxury made for the Emperor and his family; it was also enjoyed by the wealthy, but became widespread after travelers along the Silk Road stopped at tea houses during their long journeys. It was here that people discovered how dirnking tea can help with digestion, so small dishes of food were offered with the tea. This began the long tradition of yum cha.

From there, the cuisine has been developed and changed as restaurants soon competed with each other for business by offering yum cha in increasing varieties. Nowadays, yum cha is an integral part of Chinese culture. It has even gained popularity and great appreciation in many other Asian countries where they've adjusted the fillings, pastries, and shapes depending on their cultures, regions, and climates, but the practice remains relatively the same. It has been argued that the tastiest and best yum cha comes from Southern China, Canton and Hong Kong, where people divulge in this delicacy every day; it would almost be blasphemy if it wasn't a daily fare with friends or family.
